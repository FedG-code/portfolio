Eve of Destruction

A 2D metroidvania with combo-based combat, a morality system that unlocks divergent powers, and ability-gated exploration. As Technical Designer on a small team, I built the combat, enemies and movement from scratch in GDScript. Steam demo available.

Role

Technical Designer

Timeline

2024

Stack

Godot, GDScript

01 - Combat System

Combat

I wanted combos to feel like the player was committing to attacks rather than mashing through them. Each attack is a state in a finite state machine, and I used input buffering so the next hit only fires when the current attack's window closes. This helps lock players into combos, making spamming more punishing and comitting to a string of attacks an important consideration.

On the second hit the player can branch: neutral continues the standard combo, up launches enemies airborne for air follow-ups, and horizontal triggers a forward lunge. I wanted mid-combo decisions that felt intuitive without needing complex inputs. Each branch is just a direction hold during the buffer window.

Berserk mode swaps the moveset entirely, giving the player a parallel combo chain with a heavy finisher. The player also deals damage while entering and exiting berserk mode. Given that the ultimate bar fills easily and triggers automatically, this helped players not feel punished when it triggers mid fight.

02 - Enemies

Enemies

Crystal Eaters & Stars

Crystal Eaters are the game's main grunt, but I wanted them to feel coordinated rather than mindless. They run on a ten-state FSM with three layered detection ranges, and nearby Crystal Eaters track each other's positions to avoid crowding the player. In practice this means they fan out and approach from different angles, which forces the player to reposition rather than just standing and swinging. Stars are simpler but both enemy types share the same component architecture for damage, health, and drops, with balance values in external resource files so the designer could tune them without touching code.

Boss: Sword-and-Shield Knight

I wanted the knight to feel unpredictable on any given attack but learnable over time. It picks between thrusts, overhead strikes, and a branching multi-hit combo using weighted randomization, so the player can't just memorize a fixed pattern. After every three committed attacks it drops into a recovery window, which gives the fight a rhythm the player can exploit once they recognize it.

The shield added an interesting positional layer. It checks the knight's facing direction against the incoming hit in the shared damage component, so attacks on the shielded side bounce off with distinct VFX instead of dealing damage. The player has to work around it or wait for an opening.

03 - Movement & Traversal

Movement

The dash was the trickiest part of the movement system to get right. It needed to work as both a combat dodge and a traversal tool, and those two uses pull in opposite directions. Too fast and enemies become trivial, too much endlag and platforming feels sluggish. I tuned the speed and recovery frames separately for grounded and airborne states so it could serve both roles.
