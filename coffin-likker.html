01 — Coffin-Likker In Development

Light is your only window

Vision system screenshot

Vision & Visibility

The core mechanic inverts the typical stealth formula. Instead of hiding in darkness, the player controls a flashlight-like vision cone that determines what they can see. Custom HLSL shaders repurpose URP's Blend Style 3 as a visibility mask — entities outside the light have zero alpha, making them truly invisible rather than hidden behind fog or overlay.

A dual-threshold trigger system creates a deliberate mismatch between visual and gameplay boundaries. Shadows appear before you see the object, but you can glimpse an enemy at the cone's edge without it reacting — a grace zone that feels fair without breaking immersion.

The art direction reinforces the mechanic: a monochrome palette of black, grey, white, and red. When gameplay is about what you can and can't see, colour becomes noise — red is reserved exclusively for danger.

Enemy AI screenshot

Enemy AI System

Four distinct enemy types built with Unity Behavior Graph and custom C# action nodes. A standard patrol unit, a Weeping Angel that freezes when the player can see it, an aquatic enemy that lurks underwater and surfaces to attack, and a ranged variant that fires layer-filtered projectiles.

Detection is fully independent from player visibility — enemies can detect the player even when the player can't see them. The EnemyManager polls an array of modular AwarenessDetector components (vision cones, proximity, line-of-sight) at a configurable interval, keeping the system extensible.

Combat & Sound Propagation

Firing a weapon spawns a projectile and a sound ring. The ring uses Physics2D.OverlapCircleAll to alert nearby enemies, bridging the combat and AI systems. Every shot is a tactical decision — it reveals your position to anything listening. Per-enemy sensory toggles (deaf, blind) let level design create enemies that only respond to specific stimuli.

Water surface effects, procedural reed systems, and environmental interactions are also in active development — building out the world layer by layer.

02 — Game Jam

Tetrarcana

Tetrarcana gameplay screenshot

A 1v1 board game where two players compete to cover a shared grid with tetris-shaped pieces. Territory control works like Splatoon — overlap your opponent's pieces to claim their space. First to roughly 60% coverage wins.

Designed the entire gameplay mechanic, coded it, and worked on the visual design with an artist. Built in a two-week game jam using Unity 6.
