01 - Spin & Win+

Spin & Win+

Spin & Win+ is Logifuture's crowning game and the second most played game on the whole website. It was created as a Pixi version of the website's long time most popular game. On Spin & Win+ I was trusted with the ownership of the zoom in, free spin and small win features. The zoom in is essential, it serves to increase the tension of the game and make wins OR losses feel exciting. It kicks in when the wheel's speed drops below a threshold rather than on a fixed timer, so it always feels tied to the momentum of the spin. The background darkens around the zoom window to create a tunnel-vision effect that pulls the player's focus right to where the pin stops.

For the free spins I opted to have the background transition between all the colours already used on the wheel and increased the rate and intensity of the background star blinking feature. The colour transitions use CIELAB blending so every in-between shade stays as rich and vibrant. As the name implies players are getting another entire bet for free and they may or may not actually be able to make money off it. By using such strong visuals, we can make the player feel like a winner no matter what the actual outcome.

02 - Mini Games

Sportsbook Mini Games

After the release of Spin & Win+ I realised there was an opportunity to get some cross product synergy between casino and sportsbook off the tailend of Spin & Win+'s success. Working with an artist I changed the sizing of spin & win+, adjusted the UI and centered the bet button to make a smaller version of the game. At which point I pitched it to my managers. Within 3 sprints we released it on live and got 170k bets on launch week. 70% of the bets were from sportsbook users who had never played casino before.

After the success of the first game, two more were built, with the current, most successful one, being Take the shot mini. This game was chosen for the product synergy with sports betting. The struggle with this game was that it runs on PIXI3D real 3D models and lighting inside a 2D engine so resizing it wasn't as simple as scaling a few sprites. This game reach 220k bets on launch week.

03 - Sweet Smash

Sweet Smash

Sweet Smash is an obvious clone of Candy Crush. When the game released it was averaging 10k bets a day. I was tasked with speeding up the animations across the board and implement a skip functionality, to try and improve user engagement. While I was working on it, I realised that the game wasn't accurately communicating to the player how much they were winning (especially since larger blocks are bigger wins), nor was the game celebrating the player enough for winning when they weren't getting several cascading block breaks.

To address this, I added a simple scaling multiplier that pops up in the middle of the breaking blocks. That solved all the issues at once. Combined with the other improvements, when we released the update a sprint and a half later the game reached 60K bets a day, a 6x increase in user engagement.

6x Engagement
1.5 Sprints

04 - Fruit Slash

Fruit Slash

Fruit slash was the first game I worked on at Logifuture. Since our user's devices are very low end and run on slow networks lag is always a huge concern, especially since we're connecting it to real money transactions. Fruits have to hang in the air when the network slows down, I added a little jiggle that stopped the game from feeling frozen. The win container flying into the balance was my first time implementing a satisfying win animation using GSAP. I took that animation all the way to Spin & Win+.
