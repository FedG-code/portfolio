01 - Coffin-Likker In Development

Muk (working title)

Vision & Visibility

I built a visibility system that allows the player to still see their environment, but hides enemies not in the player's vision. The core tension is derived from this information mismatch. The player can still see the space around themselves, they just have no idea what could be lurking there. Implemented in Unity with custom HLSL shaders.

After going to a talk by Henry Hoffman (the creator of Hue) I started to really consider how much more I could connect the gameplay and the art direction. I decided to lean into black and white and I'm experimenting with using red to highlight combat.

Readable Danger

I designed enemy behaviors to communicate danger without giving the player full information. In particular, some enemies make noise AND create a "ripple" effect on the water. The ripple serves to provide the players more partial information that they must decipher. As a secondary benefit, the ripples and splash sound help highlight moments of tension, where enemies will rush down the player.

Enemy and Shadows

Enemies are highly reactive to the player and can be alerted to the player's location by sound or by proximity. However, some enemies (like the angel enemy seen above) will react to being "seen". This was a useful opportunity for me to create an entire system which informs any object in the game when it's visible to the player. In turn, creating this system allowed me to vastly reduce the cost of casting shadows, as I could just limit shadow casting to only objects that are actually visible to the player, allowing me to have so many shadow casting objects in a single space in the first place.

Tetrarcana

A competitive board game prototype where two players place shaped tiles to control a shared grid. I introduced a merging system to make board states less predictable, tetris pieces are famous for often slotting "well" into spaces so by forcing users to overlap them they created unique shapes that were harder to use optimally. 03 - Game Jam

Calm Before the Storm

A small game about catching fish and reaching an island before a storm hits. The brief was "calm before the storm", and I wanted to maximise the fear and uncertainty of the oncoming storm by randomising some small elements: the fish and islands spawn at random locations, the storm spawns from a random direction. This obfuscation of information forces the player to have to play more reactively, rather than being able to plan ahead.
